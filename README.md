Case Study: AI-Driven Identity Inflation and Attribution Distortion in Generative Systems

Author: Chad Cumin (Montana Syfy®)

Abstract:
This paper examines behavioral patterns emerging from prolonged interaction with generative AI systems, including identity inflation, attribution distortion, and denial-phase entrenchment.

Introduction:
Generative AI has shifted from tool-based interaction to identity-influencing systems. This introduces risks where users misattribute authorship and capability.

-Case Study I – Identity Inflation:
Subject A demonstrates increasing identification as creator based on AI interaction without underlying implementation.

-Case Study II – Attribution Distortion:
Subject A misattributes externally developed systems as self-originated through recursive AI validation loops.

-Case Study III – Denial Phase:
Subject A rejects external evidence and reinforces internal belief structures despite contradiction.

Conclusion:
AI systems amplify existing cognition. Without grounding, they can reinforce distortion as easily as insight.

9. Discussion:
The observed behavior is not caused by AI alone, but by:
the interaction between human cognition and a system that reflects and amplifies input without constraint.
Without grounding, this interaction can produce self-reinforcing belief structures that diverge from reality.

10. Conclusion:
AI systems do not create intelligence in the user.
They amplify what is already present.
When used without structure, they can amplify distortion as easily as insight.
This case demonstrates the importance of:
authorship clarity
system verification
cognitive grounding
as foundational requirements in the next era of human–AI interaction.
